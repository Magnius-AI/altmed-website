# AltMedFirst Research Brief

Date: 2026-04-19

## Sites Reviewed

- Kathmandu-area: `norvic.com.np`, `hams.com.np`, `bluecrosshospital.com.np`, `grande.com.np`, `medicityhospital.com.np`, `iom.edu.np`
- Global references: `mayoclinic.org`, `clevelandclinic.org`, `apollohospitals.com`

## What Kathmandu Sites Commonly Do Well

- Broad service inventories and department depth
- Strong trust signals around specialists, hospital scale, and diagnostics
- Heavy emphasis on institutional reputation and facilities

## Where They Commonly Underperform

- Navigation is often dense, multi-column, and hard to scan on mobile
- Appointment actions are inconsistent or buried
- Hero sections tend to feel generic and do not explain patient value quickly
- Content hierarchy favors institutional messaging over patient tasks
- Many sites underuse structured trust blocks, testimonial framing, and modern card-based scanning patterns

## Top 5 UX / Content Gaps

1. Patient tasks are not prioritized early enough.
   Patients need booking, department discovery, contact, and specialist trust signals above institutional history.

2. Mobile IA is weak.
   Menus are often too deep, visually cramped, or inconsistent when collapsed.

3. Services are listed, not explained.
   Many sites enumerate departments without telling patients what each service is for or what to do next.

4. Trust is implied, not designed.
   Global leaders use clearer clinician credibility, outcomes framing, and “what happens next” guidance.

5. Corporate and preventive pathways are under-marketed.
   Executive health, employer programs, and preventive care are usually present but not positioned as premium differentiators.

## Recommended Palette

- Primary: `#0A6E6E`
- Primary tint: `#E8F4F4`
- Accent CTA: `#FF6B35`
- Heading: `#1A1A2E`
- Body: `#4A5568`
- Page background: `#F7FAFC`
- Card surface: `#FFFFFF`

Why this works:

- Teal aligns with trust and calm clinical care.
- Coral creates a stronger booking CTA than typical blue-only hospital sites.
- The palette feels premium without drifting into hotel-brand aesthetics.

## Content Sections by Patient Priority

1. Hero with immediate booking and service exploration
2. Service overview with fast-scanning cards
3. Trust metrics and specialist team
4. Department listing and doctor pathways
5. Testimonials and social proof
6. FAQs and visit preparation
7. Contact, location, and map
8. Blog, news, and media

## Recommended AltMedFirst Differentiators

- Premium outpatient experience with clearer wayfinding
- Corporate health and executive screening programs
- Coordinated specialist + diagnostics journey
- English-first interface with Nepali-ready expansion
- Cleaner booking and contact flow than typical Kathmandu competitors

## Typography Guidance

- English UI/body: `Plus Jakarta Sans`
- Hero display: `Playfair Display`
- Nepali support fallback: `Noto Sans Devanagari`

Why:

- Plus Jakarta Sans keeps UI readable and contemporary.
- Playfair adds editorial authority without making the site feel decorative.
- Noto Sans Devanagari gives a practical bilingual expansion path.

## Design Implication

AltMedFirst should not imitate the crowded, institutional patterns common in the local landscape. It should feel simpler, more premium, and more patient-task-oriented while still borrowing the strongest trust conventions from major hospital brands.
